Rishab Balasubramanian

I am currently a first year MS student at Oregon State University, advised by Prasad Tadepalli. I work on 3D computer vision, and object reconstruction from images, adn have been trying my hand at reinforcement learning. Previously I was working as a Data Scientist/Computer Vision Engineer at EndovisionAi. Here I worked on SLAM and Shape from Shading methods for reconstructing human anatomy using endoscope images. I was a Research Assistant at MARMoT Laboratory, and the MOON Laboratory. I completed my Bachelor's (B.Tech) from the National Institute of Technology Tiruchirapalli (NIT-Trichy), India, where I was a part of RMI, the official robotics club. I have also worked at the Biomedical Instrumentation and Signal Processing Lab with Prof. Ramasubba Reddy

As part of my undergraduate thesis with Prof. Umapathy, I worked on developing and comapring the results from Optimal Reciprocal Collision Avoidance (ORCA) with other traditional methods for multi-agent collision avoidance and formation control. I am mainly interested in control and dynamics, path planning, optimization and learning for robots.

Research

My primary research lies in the area of 3D computer vision. I work on Shape-from-Shading, Shape-from-Motion, SLAM, and object reconstruction using Deep Learning. I'm also extensively interested in robotics, where these ideas may be applied. I used to work on developing optimal control and path planning algorithms for mobile and legged robots robots. My robotics journey began with electronics, and moved on to deep learning and vision, and finally to control and path-planning algorithms. Over the past few years I have worked on SLAM, Reinforcement Learning, control and algorithms. Im also interested in competitive programming (just started xD)
